Professor Wangari Maathai

Kenyan environmental political activist and Nobel laureate

Professor Wangari Maathai

Here is a timeline of Professor Wangari Maathai's life:

  • 1940: Born in 1940 in Nyeri District, Kenya.
  • 1943: relocated to a White-owned farm in the Rift Valley, near the town of Nakuru, where her father had found work.
  • 1951: Moved to St. Cecilia's Intermediate Primary School, a boarding school at the Mathari Catholic Mission in Nyeri.
  • 1960: She was selected among 300 other Kenyans to study in the United States. She received a scholarship to study at Mount St. Scholastica College (now Benedictine College), in Atchison, Kansas, where she majored in biology, with minors in chemistry and German
  • 1966: Received her M.Sc in biological sciences and was appointed to a position as research assistant to a professor of zoology at University College of Nairobi.
  • 1967: She traveled to the University of Giessen in Germany in pursuit of a doctorate.
  • 1969: Returned to Nairobi to continue studies at the University College of Nairobi as an assistant lecturer. She also became the first Eastern African woman to receive a PhD, her doctorate in veterinary anatomy from the University College of Nairobi, which became the University of Nairobi the following year.
  • 1971: She became the first Eastern African woman to receive a PhD, her doctorate in veterinary anatomy from the University of Nairobi.
  • 1977: Founded the Green Belt Movement, an environmental non-governmental organization focused on the planting of trees, environmental conservation, and women's rights.
  • 1997: She campaigned for parliament as a presidential candidate of the Liberal Party of Kenya but lost the election.
  • 2002: Campaigned for parliament, this time as a candidate of the National Rainbow Coalition, the umbrella organization which finally united the opposition. She won the Tetu Constituency seat with an overwhelming 98% of the vote.
  • 2003: Was appointed Assistant Minister in the Ministry for Environment and Natural Resources and served in that capacity until November 2005. She also founded the Mazingira Green Party of Kenya in to allow candidates to run on a platform of conservation as embodied by the Green Belt Movement.
  • 2004: Won the Nobel Peace Prize and became the first African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize for her contribution to sustainable development, democracy and peace.
  • 2011: Dies at a tender age of 71.

“ Maathai stood up courageously against the former oppressive regime in Kenya. Her unique forms of action have contributed to drawing attention to political oppression—nationally and internationally. She has served as inspiration for many in the fight for democratic rights and has especially encouraged women to better their situation. ”
— The Norwegian Nobel Committee, in a statement announcing her as the 2004 Nobel Peace Prize winner.
